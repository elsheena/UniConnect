Study in Russia & Platform FAQs

Find answers to common questions about migrating to Russia, managing academic student life, and using the UniConnect ecosystem.

Legal Status (Russia)
What is RVPO (РВПО) and how do I apply for it?
RVPO (Разрешение на временное проживание в целях получения образования) is a Temporary Residence Permit for Education. International students studying full-time in state-accredited Bachelor's, Specialist's, Master's, or PhD programs are eligible.

Benefits:
  • Valid for the duration of your studies plus an additional 180 days.
  • Eliminates the need to renew your study visa every year.
  • Allows you to work legally in Russia without a patent or separate work permit.
  • Entitles you to apply for permanent residence (VNZH / ВНЖ) immediately after graduation.
How to apply: Apply at your local migration center (e.g., the Sakharovo Center in Moscow) with your passport, a notarized translation, your university enrollment certificate, your study contract, health screening clearance, and a fingerprint card.
Living in Russia
What are the housing options (Dormitory vs. Renting) and registration rules?
Foreign students have two main options:

1. University Dormitories: These are highly affordable, ranging from $15 to $50 per month. They are usually situated near campus and provide shared rooms with laundry and kitchen facilities.
2. Private Apartments: Renting a studio or flat costs between $300 and $700 per month depending on the city (e.g. Moscow/St. Petersburg vs. regional cities).

Migration Registration Notice: Whether you live in a dorm or rent, you must be registered with the migration service (Регистрация) within 7 business days of entering Russia or changing address. Universities register dormitory residents automatically, whereas landlords must register apartment tenants.
Connectivity (Russia)
How do I purchase a local SIM card?
You can purchase a SIM card at official telecommunication shops (salons) belonging to major Russian providers: MTS, Megafon, Beeline, or Tele2.

Requirements: You must present your original passport along with your migration card (issued at border control). You cannot buy a SIM card using copies or without a valid entry status.

Tip: Many operators offer special discounts or unlimited social media plans tailored specifically for students. Ask for "Student tariffs" when registering.
Finance (Russia)
How do I open a Russian bank account and debit card?
Due to international sanctions, foreign-issued Visa and Mastercard cards do not work in Russia. Opening a local bank account is essential for daily transactions.

How to open: Visit any major bank branch (Sberbank, Tinkoff, Alfa-Bank, or VTB) with:
  • Your original passport.
  • A notarized Russian translation of your passport (often required).
  • Your student visa and migration registration document.
You will receive a local debit card running on the national MIR payment system, which can be managed via mobile applications.
Health & Safety
Is health insurance mandatory for foreign students?
Yes, Voluntary Medical Insurance (DMS / ДМС) is strictly mandatory for all international students from the very first day they arrive in the Russian Federation.

A standard DMS policy typically costs between $60 and $120 per year. It covers primary clinical services, outpatient diagnostics, emergency dental work, emergency ambulance assistance, and hospitalization. You can buy a DMS policy directly through your university's international office or from verified insurance representatives.
Employment (Russia)
Can international students work legally in Russia?
Yes. According to Federal Law No. 16, international students enrolled in full-time state-accredited higher education programs can work in Russia without needing a work patent or standard work permit.

Requirements:
  • You must be a full-time student.
  • You need to present an official reference certificate (Справка об обучении) from your university to your employer.
  • You must sign a standard employment contract (Трудовой договор).
There are no restrictions on working hours, but the job must not interfere with your class attendance.
Entry (Russia)
What is a Migration Card and why is it critical?
The Migration Card (Миграционная карта) is a small paper form handed out on the plane/train or generated at border passport control when entering Russia.

Crucial Detail: You must state the purpose of entry as "Education" (Учеба). If you enter with "Tourism" (Туризм) or "Private" (Частный) marked, the university will not be legally allowed to register you or extend your student visa. You would have to exit the country and re-enter.

Keep this card safe in your passport. Losing it requires visiting the migration police within 3 days for a duplicate.
Platform Ecosystem
What documents are required to register as a verified Student?
To obtain the Verified Student badge, you must upload a clear copy of a document confirming your enrollment in a Russian higher education institution. Accepted files include your student ID card (Студенческий билет), official enrollment certificate (Справка об обучении), or university admission letter. An administrator will review your files within 24 hours.
Platform Services
How do I book an Airport Pickup service?
Applicants can browse the Services tab, select the Airport Pickup option, enter their arrival date, airport, and flight number, and submit the booking. The booking will appear in the open feed for verified students. Once a student accepts your request, a private 1-on-1 chat tunnel will open to let you coordinate details.
Platform Finance
What are Matryoshka Points (MP) and how do I earn them?
Matryoshka Points (MP) are the internal platform reward currency. Verified students earn MP by claiming service requests (e.g. consultations or airport pickups) and completing them. Once both parties confirm completion, the points are credited to the student's wallet, which can later be withdrawn or transferred.
Platform Community
How do I join my national country group or university chat?
These groups are gated to ensure safety. Once your registration is approved and verified by an admin, the system automatically parses your Nationality and University fields and joins you to your respective Country Group (e.g. Egyptians in Russia) and University Chat. You can then access them from the Groups tab.
Platform Services
Is the first student consultation really free?
Yes, the first consultation for any applicant user is 100% free. This allows you to connect with a verified student at your target university to ask initial questions without any financial commitment. Subsequent consultations or specialized university representative calls are priced according to the rates shown on the service detail cards.
